# Firewall & Network Security Course

*One of the best courses I had during my time at university — not just because of the content, but because of the group.*

---

## The Course

The course covered firewall design and network security from the ground up. Each lab (or *case*) had two parts: the hands-on technical work, and a written **company report** documenting what you did, why you did it, and what it meant from a real-world security perspective. The idea was to simulate how an actual team inside a company would plan, implement, and document their network security decisions.

---

## The Group

Most group work at university is painful because you get stuck with people who disappear before the deadline or you end up carrying the whole thing yourself like its a CS2 match. This was different. We all clicked from day one. Everyone showed up, everyone contributed, and everyone actually cared about the outcome. That combination is rarer than it sounds, and it made the entire course a genuinely good time.

---

## The Approach: Fuck Around and Find Out

Our working method wasn't exactly textbook. We'd dive into a lab headfirst, break things, figure out why they broke, fix them, break them again in a different way, and eventually land on something solid. By the time we wrote the report, we actually understood what we'd built not because we'd followed a guide step by step, but because we'd hit every wall along the way. We fucked around and found out.

What was funny to watch in hindsight was how the reports evolved. The first ones were pretty rough functional, but clearly written by people who just wanted to document *something* and move on. By the later cases, we had a whole flow going: structured sections, proper reasoning, actual recommendations. The "fuck around and find out" approach quietly matured into something that looked surprisingly professional. Also the course was some kind of competition between groups, each week a new group won based on their submission. Important note, our group ever so far to have won 3 times for as long as the course has been going.

---
